Gates and Allen began building Microsoft’s future long before it became a global giant. In the mid-1970s, the company’s first headquarters sat in Albuquerque, New Mexico, a modest office where the two innovators worked side by side.

It was there that they developed early versions of Microsoft BASIC, secured their first major deals, and laid the foundation for what would become one of the most influential technology companies in history. Despite its humble setting, the Albuquerque office marked the birthplace of their shared vision and the start of Microsoft’s rise

Bill Gates and Paul Allen remained at the company headquarters in Albuquerque until 1979, when they decided to relocate Microsoft to Bellevue, Washington. This move was strategic, allowing them to be closer to their roots and tap into the burgeoning tech scene in the Pacific Northwest.

The relocation marked a new chapter for Microsoft, setting the stage for its rapid growth and eventual dominance in the software industry.
