Giving up the CEO Role (January 2000)

At the height of the antitrust legal battles, Gates stepped down as CEO of Microsoft in January 2000.

  • The Successor: He handed the reins to his college friend, Steve Ballmer, who had been with the company since 1980.
  • New Role: Gates didn't leave; he created a new title for himself: "Chief Software Architect." This allowed him to focus purely on product strategy and code without the burden of running the business side

The End of Daily Work (June 2008)

Gates stepped down from his full-time day-to-day role at Microsoft.

  • The Reason: He wanted to dedicate the majority of his time to the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.
  • The "Last Day": His final full-time day was June 27, 2008. Microsoft released a famous comedy video for his retirement featuring celebrities like George Clooney and Bono trying to find Gates a new hobby.
  • Status: He remained Chairman of the Board, meaning he still led the group that fired/hired the CEO.

Stepping Down as Chairman (February 2014)

When Steve Ballmer retired and Satya Nadella was named the new CEO, Gates stepped down as Chairman of the Board.

  • New Role: He took the title "Technology Advisor," promising to help Nadella with product development. This was largely symbolic, signaling that the "Gates/Ballmer Era" was officially over and it was now Nadella's company.

The Final Departure (March 2020)

Gates resigned from the Microsoft Board of Directors entirely.

  • Offical Reason He stated he wanted to dedicate his time fully to philanthropy (global health, education, and climate change).
  • Behind The Scenes In 2021, The Wall Street Journal reported that before his resignation, the board had hired a law firm to investigate a romantic relationship Gates had with an employee years prior. Gates resigned before the investigation was concluded, though his spokesperson said the decision to leave was unrelated to the inquiry.
