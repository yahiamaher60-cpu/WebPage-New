The Transition to Philanthropy (Since 2000)

In 2000, Bill Gates stepped down from his day-to-day role at Microsoft to focus on philanthropy through the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, which he co-founded with his then-wife Melinda. The foundation is one of the largest private philanthropic organizations in the world, with a mission to enhance healthcare, reduce extreme poverty, and expand educational opportunities and access to information technology.

Vision and Mission

  • Global Health: Eradicating diseases like malaria and polio, and improving maternal and child health.
  • Global Development: Improving sanitation, sustainable agriculture, and access to finance for the poor.
  • US Education: Improving quality of education and student outcomes in the United States.
  • Gavi, the Vaccine Alliance: A public-private partnership to increase access to immunization in poor countries.

Foundation Success

In 2006, Warren Buffett announced an ongoing gift to the foundation. As of 2025, about 41% of the foundation’s funds have come from Buffett, with the remainder drawn from Gates’s Microsoft fortune. Together, these donations helped the foundation emerge as a uniquely powerful force in global health and education—what Gates has called his “second and final career.”
